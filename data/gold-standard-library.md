## Phase 6: Close — Best Objection Handling (Partner/Co-Parent Isolation)

### Anthony — Judith Lacey (CLOSED, March 23) — Partner Objection Isolation ⭐
> Anthony: "If dad wasn't on board, what would happen?"
> Judith: [Explains she would still want to move forward]
> Anthony: [Proceeds to value confirmation] "On a scale of one to ten?"
> Judith: "Oh, ten. Ten for sure."

**Why it works:** Anthony didn't try to argue dad's case or overcome the objection directly. He isolated the conditional ("if dad wasn't here, would you?") to get Judith's own YES on record first. Once the mum says "I would still go ahead" — the objection is exposed as a delay tactic, not a real blocker. This is the cleanest partner objection isolation in the scored call library.

**When to use:** Any time "I need to check with [dad/partner]" surfaces. The sequence: isolate ("if they were 100% on board, are WE good to go?") → get conditional YES → THEN address the partner buy-in as a logistics problem, not a re-decision.

## Phase-by-Phase: Anthony vs Harry Pattern Comparison

| Dimension | Harry (Kelly) | Anthony (Best - Judith) | Anthony (Typical - Sophie) |
|-----------|--------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Discovery silence | 4+ min uninterrupted | 2-3 min, some redirects | 1-2 min, frequent questions |
| Deepest question | "What worries you most?" | Same question, lands well | Asks but doesn't follow up |
| When mum reveals fear | Stays with it, honours it | Acknowledges, moves on | "Of course" + redirect |
| Pain stack | 50% son, 50% mum | 70% son, 30% mum | 80% son, 20% mum |
| Close approach | Built during deepening | Built during pitch | Attempted at close only |
| Objection handling | Pre-handled via depth | Gets lucky (10/10 leads) | Accepts without isolating |
| Name usage | 15-20+ per call | 10-15 per call | 8-12 per call |

### The Core Gap
Anthony's structure is solid — he follows the script. But he treats discovery and deepening as checkboxes ("ask fear question ✅, move to pitch") rather than as the actual sale. Harry's deepening IS the close. By the time Harry mentions price, the mum has already decided. Anthony mentions price and then has to sell.

**The fix is one habit:** When the mum says something heavy, instead of acknowledging and moving on, ask ONE more question about it. Just one. "Tell me more about that." "What does that feel like for you?" "How long have you been carrying that?" That one extra question is the difference between a 5/10 and an 8/10 call.

## Phase 5: Pitch — Son Buy-In Pre-Handle (Dentist Metaphor)

### Tom — Bec Richardson (CLOSED, March 26) ⭐ NEW GOLD STANDARD
> Tom: "It's a bit like getting them to the dentist, just get them in the chair and they'll get it done. You get them on the phone with us and boys that were reluctant will be buzzing for the next call. So yeah, it's probably more importantly, who we're not that helps the boys connect than who we are."

**Context:** Bec had pre-empted son buy-in concern — "He'll be anti it to start with." Tom didn't wait for it to become an objection. He absorbed her concern naturally and reframed before moving on.

**Why it works:**
1. Pre-emptive — delivered before Bec had to push on it
2. The dentist metaphor is concrete, relatable, un-threatening
3. "Who we're not" framing is brilliant — positions B&G against every failed previous support without naming them
4. Bec responded: "Yeah, I can see that" — full buy-in, zero resistance

**Grade: 5/5** — best Son Buy-In handle in the scored library. Bar previously not set in gold standard.
**When to use:** Any time the mum reveals the son is resistant, reluctant, or anti-help. Deliver mid-pitch, not at close. Make it casual, not scripted.
**Contrast:** This is the difference between pre-handling (Tom) and reactive handling. Don't wait for the objection — weave it in.

## Harry's Unique Techniques (Kelly Wellsmore — Full Read, March 26)

### The "Regardless" Frame (Phase 1)
> "Regardless, I hope you feel better for this call and it's valuable for you either way."

**Why it works:** Removes the transactional pressure immediately. The call becomes about the mum, not about the sale. She relaxes. Discovery opens up.

### The Male Role Model Probe (Phase 2)
> "And Kelly, you mentioned like Kurt, your current partner but has there or is there that you're aware of anyone that you feel as though Jackson looks up to in terms of male role models?"

**Why it works:** Harry doesn't assume the answer. He asks specifically and directly. The answer shapes the pitch framing — if there's no male role model at all, the Bridge metaphor hits harder. If there is one, Harry references them positively.

### On Medication/Diagnosis (Phase 2) — Handling Complex Histories
Harry never commented on medication, diagnosis, or psychological history judgmentally. When Kelly mentioned ODD, ADHD, fluoxetine, psychiatrists — Harry simply absorbed it and used it to build picture. He never said "of course" and redirected. He let her run.

### The "Wearing All These Hats" Stack (Phase 3)
> "What causes or what is probably causing you the most stress? Is it trying to wear all these hats as mum?"

**Why it works:** Harry introduces the "wearing hats" language in the question. Kelly adopts it. Then he uses it again in the summary: "you're trying to wear all these hats as mom, dad, and friend." Her language, reflected back. She hears herself.

### Acknowledging Without Solving (Phase 3) — Harry's Signature
> Kelly: "I feel helpless. I feel like I can't... I just don't know what to do. I'm just hoping for the best which is a really slack strategy."
> Harry: "Well, look, especially Kelly, I really appreciate you sharing all that."

Harry NEVER said "that's understandable" or offered a solution here. He just validated the courage to share it. The mum doesn't need solutions in deepening - she needs to feel heard.

### Payment Link Speed (Phase 6) — The Real Close Signal
> Harry presents $111, sends link while still on the call
> Kelly: "Yep, I've just done that."
> Harry: "Oh, you already put it through? Yeah."

Harry sends the payment link on the call, not after. This is non-negotiable. Anthony often says "I'll send it through after we hang up" — which is a conversion killer. By the time they hang up, the mum has had time to pause, and the urgency is gone. Send it. On. The. Call.

### FE Seeding After Close (Post-Phase 6)
> "I wouldn't usually even plant the seed and maybe it's something we just check back in around, you know, four weeks mark... we do like a Founder's Experience program"

Harry seeds FE AFTER the close, not during the pitch. Timing matters — the mum has already said yes, she's in a good feeling state. It's a natural escalation from a position of trust, not a competing option that muddies the main sale.

### The Community Close (Phase 5/6 Bridge)
> "I don't want you to feel like you're on the sidelines just watching. I want you to feel like you've got a relationship with Jackson's mentor too, Kelly"

Harry speaks directly to the mum's isolation — she's been doing this alone for 15 years. The B&G community (parent calls, group sessions) isn't a feature list item. Harry frames it as: *you won't be alone anymore.* That hits differently.
